# ICML Introduction Phrases

Ready-to-use phrases extracted from 10 ICML 2025 oral papers. Organized by function.

## Opening Phrases

### Establishing Importance
- "X is fundamental to..."
- "X serves as the foundation for..."
- "X remains a significant challenge..."
- "X are driving many facets of..."
- "A crucial requirement for such systems is..."

### Establishing Context
- "In contrast to X, Y focuses on..."
- "The dynamic interaction among X creates..."
- "As X grow in scale, Y becomes increasingly valuable..."
- "X traditionally have been evaluated on..."

### Signaling Evolution
- "With increasing X and Y, Z have shown substantial improvements..."
- "Recent work shows that..."
- "State-of-the-art models have outgrown the usefulness of..."

## Problem Framing Phrases

### Importance
- "The essence of X lies in..."
- "holds significant potential in many areas such as..."
- "The study of X is crucial for understanding..."
- "Understanding this is crucial for..."

### Challenge/Difficulty
- "X have remained particularly challenging for..."
- "effective management of X is becoming extremely challenging"
- "This becomes salient in the common case in which..."
- "These challenges are compounded by..."

### Scale/Scope
- "even if X is robust to Y, Z may be able to..."
- "This incident underlined the critical need for..."

---

## Gap Identification Phrases

### Technical Limitations
- "...their applicability to X is constrained by..."
- "...incur significant computational costs, especially for..."
- "the computational cost linked to..."
- "Unfortunately, several of the X studied by Y are not available in practice"

### Prior Work Limitations
- "While these efforts significantly contribute to X, Y remains underexplored"
- "However, its limited scope... leaves critical questions unanswered"
- "This approach has limitations, as X does not always lead to Y"
- "X represents the first Y... However, its limited scope..."

### Historical Framing
- "Despite its simple statement, this problem has resisted resolution for..."
- "Despite... this remains..."

### Empirical Gap
- "On X... Y\% success. But on Z... only W\%"

- "In contrast,"
- "Nevertheless,"
- "Unlike X, Y..."
- "While X, Y..."

### Addition
- "Furthermore,"
- "Moreover,"
- "Additionally,"
- "In addition,"

### Sequence
- "First, ... Second, ... Third, ..."
- "We first... We then..."
- "Subsequently,"

### Cause/Effect
- "Therefore,"
- "Thus,"
- "Consequently,"
- "As a result,"
- "Hence,"

### Evidence
- "Specifically,"
- "In particular,"
- "For instance,"
- "As shown in..."

### Solution Signal
- "To address this challenge, we..."
- "To address these questions, we introduce..."
- "In this paper, we..."
- "This paper addresses this critical need..."
- "Given the impracticality of X, a natural alternative is..."

## Solution Preview Phrases

### Framework Introduction
- "We present a framework that reformulates..."
- "We introduce X, a Y for Z..."
- "We propose X, which..."
- "Building on these insights, we present..."

### Problem Reframing
- "We build a bridge between X and Y"
- "This continuous formulation enables us to..."
- "The transformation features a straightforward..."

### Mechanism Explanation
- "This X enables us to..."
- "This is possible in large part because..."

### Scope Clarification
- "Importantly, X do not constitute Y but rather provide guidance..."
- "Our approach differs from X in that..."

## Contribution Phrases

### Introduction
- "Our major contributions are as follows:"
- "Our main contributions are:"
- "Our contributions are threefold:"
- "We make the following contributions:"
- "Our key contributions include:"

### Action Verbs for Contributions
- "We propose / introduce / present..."
- "We demonstrate / show / prove..."
- "We achieve / obtain / establish..."
- "We provide / develop / design..."
- "We conduct / perform / evaluate..."

### Quantified Claims
- "achieves X\% accuracy, a Y\% improvement over..."
- "requires only Xms, Y times faster than..."
- "improves on the previous best of X to Y"
- "reduces X from Y to Z"

### Scope Claims
- "To the best of our knowledge, this is the first..."
- "first of its kind framework"
- "the first benchmark to..."

## Hedging Language

### Strong Certainty
- demonstrates
- achieves
- proves
- establishes
- shows

### Moderate Certainty
- indicates
- reveals
- suggests
- implies
- enables

### Cautious Claims
- may
- might
- appears to
- potentially
- likely
- can

### Meta-Caveats
- "Importantly, X do not constitute Y but rather provide guidance that must be Z"
- "in the X regime"
- "under the assumption that..."

## Results Preview Phrases

### Findings Introduction
- "Our evaluation yields X key findings:"
- "Results demonstrate that..."
- "Our findings show that..."
- "We find that..."

### Comparative Results
- "Compared to X, our method..."
- "outperforms X by Y\%"
- "achieves state-of-the-art performance on..."
- "significantly outperforms existing approaches"

### Insight Framing
- "Our key observation is that..."
- "This result provides a new interpretation of..."
- "These findings indicate that..."
- "This suggests that..."
